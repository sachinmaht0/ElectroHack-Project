Hygiene Tips

  • Wash hands with soap or use alcohol-based sanitizers frequently.
  • Ensure clean water and proper sanitation facilities.
  • Dispose of medical waste safely, separating hazardous materials.
  • Use and properly dispose of personal protective equipment (PPE).
  • Sterilize medical tools and equipment regularly.
  • Keep healthcare spaces clean and disinfect surfaces often

  • Isolate infectious patients to prevent disease spread.

Common Diseases

  • Malaria - Sleep under insecticide-treated nets, use insect repellents, eliminate mosquito breeding sites, and take antimalarial medications.
  • Diabetes - Eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, maintain a healthy weight, and monitor blood sugar levels.
  • Diarrheal Diseases (e.g., Cholera, Dysentery) - Drink clean water, practice food hygiene, wash hands regularly, and promote sanitation.
  • Respiratory Infections (e.g., Cold, Flu, Pneumonia) - Wash hands frequently, avoid contact with sick individuals, get vaccinated, and maintain good ventilation
  • Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) - Reduce salt intake, exercise regularly, avoid excessive alcohol and smoking, and manage stress.
  • HIV/AIDS - Practice safe sex, avoid sharing needles, get regular testing, and use antiretroviral therapy if at risk.
  • Skin Diseases (e.g., Fungal Infections, Eczema) - Maintain personal hygiene, avoid sharing personal items, keep skin clean and dry, and use medicated soaps if necessary.
